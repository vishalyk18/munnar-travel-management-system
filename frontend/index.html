7 Must-Visit Spots

1. Eravikulam National Park

Home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr, this park offers breathtaking views of rolling hills and lush grasslands.

2. Mattupetty Dam

Famous for its storage masonry dam and the beautiful lake, offering boat rides with stunning views.

3. Kolukkumalai Tea Estate

The world's highest tea plantation. Witness the majestic sunrise over the ghats.

4. Top Station

The highest point in Munnar, offering panoramic views of the Western Ghats.

5. Attukal Waterfalls

A scenic destination known for its cascading waterfalls and rolling hills.

6. Kundala Lake

Historical dam and lake, famous for Neela Kurinji flowers. Enjoy a pedal boat ride here.

7. Blossom Park

A beautiful park with vibrant flowers, underground meditation center, and adventure trails.

Let's Check The Direction

Top Station Direction

When traveling from Munnar to Top Station, there are several tourist spots along the way. Popular attractions include Mattupetty Dam, offering scenic boating and farm visits. Echo Point is celebrated for its natural echo phenomenon and mist-clad mountain views. Enjoy pedal boating at Kundala Lake, surrounded by cherry blossoms and lush greenery. The ultimate destination is Top Station itself, renowned for its panoramic views over the Western Ghats and the blooming of Neelakurinji flowers.

Thekkady Direction

A journey towards Thekkady promises encounters with pristine nature. En route, the Spice Gardens offer an educational aromatic experience. Experience the thrill of Elephant Parks and the sheer natural beauty of Chengulam Dam. Further along, you'll encounter breathtaking vistas at 2nd Mile View Point and Pothamedu View Point. For adventurous souls, adrenaline-pumping ziplines and the stunning Attukad Waterfalls make the trip unforgettable.

Echo Point Direction

On your way to Echo Point, you'll be treated to rolling tea estates and picturesque reservoirs. It sits gracefully at an altitude of 600 ft, surrounded by mist, green landscapes, and a tranquil lake. Shout as loud as you can and listen to your voice bouncing back from the hills! The calm boating experiences available here form the perfect relaxing getaway.

Chinnar Direction

The road to Chinnar is incredibly majestic, leading through the famous Marayoor Sandalwood Forests where you can explore ancient rock paintings. Enjoy the captivating beauty of Thoovanam Waterfalls hidden deep in the woods. Ultimately, you'll arrive at the Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary, home to elephants, deer, and exotic bird species. Nearby, you can also explore the fruit-bearing haven of Kanthalloor.

The best time to visit Munnar is from September to March when the weather is pleasant and ideal for trekking and sightseeing. The monsoon season (June to August) is also beautiful, showcasing lush greenery and misty landscapes.
